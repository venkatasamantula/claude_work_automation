Mentorship isn't dying — it's decentralizing. 🧭 In hybrid teams, the old "find a senior who'll adopt you" model is giving way to structured, multi-directional networks: targeted in-office sessions, async coaching channels, and digital mentorship tools that deliver guidance where the work actually happens [1]. The bigger shift is direction itself — 38% of employers now say AI expertise is essential for success, and junior employees are openly mentoring senior leaders on the tools reshaping their roles [2]. ⚡ The new mentor isn't one wise person above you; it's a small portfolio of peers, juniors, and domain experts you assemble on purpose. Stop waiting to be picked — design your mentor stack like you'd design a product.
